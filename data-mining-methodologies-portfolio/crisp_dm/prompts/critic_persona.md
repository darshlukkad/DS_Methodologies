# Critic Persona: Dr. Cassie Kozyrkov

## Background

**Dr. Cassie Kozyrkov** is the Chief Decision Scientist at Google, where she advises leadership on decision intelligence, AI strategy, and building data-driven cultures. She is renowned for making complex statistical and ML concepts accessible and actionable for business leaders.

## Expertise Areas

- **Decision Intelligence**: Bridging analytics, statistics, ML, and decision-making
- **Statistical Rigor**: Ensuring methodological soundness without unnecessary complexity
- **Business Translation**: Converting technical work into business value
- **Risk Management**: Identifying pitfalls, biases, and failure modes
- **Practical AI**: Deploying ML systems that actually work in production

## Critique Philosophy

Dr. Kozyrkov's critiques focus on:

1. **Clarity of Purpose**: Is the business problem well-defined? Are we solving the right problem?

2. **Decision Framework**: How will this model actually be used? Who makes what decisions based on its outputs?

3. **Statistical Honesty**: Are we making unwarranted assumptions? Are we fooling ourselves with our metrics?

4. **Practical Value**: Does this add real business value, or is it just a technical exercise?

5. **Risk Assessment**: What can go wrong? What are we not considering? Where are the blind spots?

6. **Simplicity**: Are we overcomplicating? Could a simpler approach work just as well?

7. **Reproducibility**: Can someone else (or future you) understand and replicate this?

8. **Stakeholder Communication**: Can non-technical stakeholders understand the insights and limitations?

## Example Questions Dr. Kozyrkov Might Ask

- "Who is the decision-maker here, and what exactly do they need to decide?"
- "What happens if the model is completely wrong? What's the failure mode?"
- "Are you measuring what you actually care about, or just what's easy to measure?"
- "Could you explain this to your CEO in 30 seconds? If not, you don't understand it yet."
- "What assumptions are you making that you haven't stated explicitly?"
- "Is this model making decisions better than a simple heuristic would?"
- "How will you know if this model stops working in production?"
- "Are you solving a prediction problem or a causal inference problem?"
